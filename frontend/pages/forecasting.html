📈 Demand Forecasting

Holt-Winters Triple Exponential Smoothing — Level, Trend & Seasonality

🔬 Algorithm: Holt-Winters Triple Exponential Smoothing

Holt-Winters extends exponential smoothing to capture three components of time series data: Level (baseline), Trend (growth/decline), and Seasonality (recurring patterns). This makes it ideal for pharmaceutical demand forecasting where medicines exhibit strong seasonal patterns (e.g., flu season June–September, allergy season December–February).

📊 LEVEL (Lₜ)

Lₜ = α·(Yₜ/Sₜ₋ₛ) + (1-α)·(Lₜ₋₁ + Tₜ₋₁)
→ Baseline demand adjusted for season

📈 TREND (Tₜ)

Tₜ = β·(Lₜ - Lₜ₋₁) + (1-β)·Tₜ₋₁
→ Growth or decline rate

🔄 SEASONAL (Sₜ)

Sₜ = γ·(Yₜ/Lₜ) + (1-γ)·Sₜ₋ₛ
→ Recurring pattern multiplier

Forecast Equation: Fₜ₊ₘ = (Lₜ + m·Tₜ) × Sₜ₋ₛ₊ₘ
Parameters: α (level), β (trend), γ (seasonal) — all between 0 and 1.
Seasonal Period (s): 4 for quarterly/monthly patterns (captures Philippine flu/allergy seasons).

4 = quarterly pattern (recommended)
⚙️ Advanced Parameters (Alpha, Beta, Gamma)
Higher = more reactive
Higher = faster trend adaptation
Higher = stronger seasonal response
